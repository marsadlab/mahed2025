Overview of the Proposed Task

We propose MAHED 2025 - Multimodal Detection of Hope and Hate Emotions in Arabic Content as a shared task for ArabicNLP 2025. This task aims to enhance Arabic NLP research by addressing the critical issue of detecting hate speech, hate memes, and promoting hope speech across text and multimodal formats, enriched with emotional insights. It includes three sub-tasks:

  • Text-based classification of hate and hope speech
  • Multitasking on Emotion, Offensive, and Hate Detection from Text
  • Multimodal detection of hate memes

Participants will have access to an annotated Arabic dataset containing both text-only and text-image content, designed to support the development of context-sensitive and robust NLP models.

Motivation for the Task

Social media amplifies hate speech (text and memes) and hope speech (positivity and resilience), especially in Arabic, where linguistic diversity and cultural nuances complicate detection. Emotions underpin these expressions but are underexplored in Arabic NLP. MAHED 2025 addresses:

  • Dual nature of online content (hate vs. hope) for moderation and positive discourse.
  • Multimodal content's rise, requiring text-image analysis.
  • Emotional context's role in intent and impact, vital for moderation, mental health, and counter-extremism.
  • Scarcity of Arabic datasets combines hate, hope, and emotions.
  • This task bridges negative and positive content analysis with emotional insights.

Data/Resource Collection and Creation

  • Dataset Status: Ready and fully annotated: Two datasets (Text dataset, and multimodal dataset) are being used for the three subtasks.
  • Text Data: There are 20,000 Arabic tweets/posts (MSA and dialects) labeled for hate/hope and emotion and are being used for sub-task 1 and sub-task 3.
  • Multimodal Data: There are 3,061 hate memes (text + images) labeled for ‘hateful’ and ‘not-hateful’ and are being used for sub-task 2. A new test set will be developed. This was previously studied in the context of Arabic memes (Alam et al., 2024).
  • Annotation: Collected from public social media, anonymized, and annotated by native speakers (Cohen’s Kappa > 0.75).
  • Data split: Data is split into training, development, and test sets with evaluation scripts, it complies with ethical data-sharing standards.
  • Sample Dataset: A sample of the dataset can be accessed through the GitHub URL: https://github.com/rafiulbiswas/MAHED25.git

Task Description

MAHED 2025 consists of three interconnected subtasks:

Sub-task 1: Text-based Hate and Hope Speech Classification

  • Input: Arabic textual content
  • Output: Labels—'hate', 'hope', or 'not_applicable'
  • Goal: Distinguish hate from hope speech and neutral content.
  • Examples:
    • Hate: "كل المهاجرين لصوص ومجرمون يجب طردهم فورا" (All immigrants are thieves and criminals; they should be expelled immediately.) - Hate
    • Hope: "معا ً يمكننا بناء مستقبل أفضل ألطفالنا" (Together, we can build a better future for our children.) - Hope
    • Not Applicable: "اليوم هو يوم مشمس وجميل" (Today is a sunny and beautiful day.) - Non-hate, Non-hope

Sub-task 2: Multitasking on Emotion, Offensive, and Hate Detection from Text

  • Input: Arabic textual content
  • Task Description: This is a multi-task classification. You have to identify Emotion from text. Then identify if the text is offensive or not. If the text is offensive, identify if it is directed to hate or not.
  • Output: Three output label
    • Emotion label: neutral, anger, anticipation, disgust, fear, joy, love, optimism, pessimism, sadness, surprise, trust
    • Offensive label: Yes/No
      • Yes → Hate Directed?
        • yes-directed
        • no-directed
      • No → No classification required
  • Goal: Integrate emotional analysis to enhance contextual understanding of offensive and hate content.
    • Examples:
    • " انتو مسويين حملة على الشوالي و تبغاه يعلق عليكم"
      (You are launching a campaign against Al-Shawali and you want him to comment on you)
      Emotion: Surprise, Offensive: Yes, Directed to Hate: yes-directed
    • "تكفون بيجيبون لي امراض مابي اشوف احد"
      (Please bring me diseases. I don't want to see anyone.)
      Emotion: fear, Offensive: No

    Sub-task 3: Multimodal Hateful Meme Detection

    • Input: Image + text pairs (Arabic memes).
    • Output: Binary label (‘hateful’ and ‘non-hateful’).
    • Goal: Detect hate in multimodal content.
    • Examples:
      • Hateful: Text: "الشعب ده ما يستاهلش غير كده" (This people don’t deserve anything but this.) + Image: Cartoon mocking a group - Hate
      • Non-hateful: Text: "لما تصحى الصبح وتالقي القهوة جاهزة" (When you wake up and find the coffee ready.) + Image: Cheerful person with coffee - Non-hate

    Participants may engage in any or all sub-tasks. The evaluation uses F1-score, precision, and recall, with macro-averaged F1 as the primary metric.

    Pilot Run Details

    A pilot study with 2,000 text instances and 500 memes yielded:

    • Sub-task 1: F1 0.78 (hate), 0.82 (hope) with BERT.
    • Sub-task 2: F1 0.75 with CLIP-style model.
    • Sub-task 3: F1 0.70 for emotion+hate/hope.

    Feedback refined the dataset; details will be shared upon acceptance.
